Siem Reap (Equinox Angkor)

4.3
(7,842)

Siem Reap Province (Khmer: ខេត្តសៀមរាប) is one of Cambodia’s most famous provinces, home to the world-renowned Angkor Archaeological Park, including Angkor Wat, Angkor Thom, and many other ancient temples. Beyond its historical treasures, the province also features natural attractions such as Phnom Kulen National Park, the floating villages on Tonle Sap Lake, and beautiful countryside landscapes. Siem Reap Province blends cultural heritage, spirituality, and natural beauty, making it a top destination for visitors.

Tonle Sap

4.3
(7,842)

Tonlé Sap (Khmer: ទន្លេសាប) is a lake in the northwest of Cambodia. Belonging to the Mekong River system, Tonlé Sap is the largest freshwater lake in Southeast Asia and one of the most diverse and productive ecosystems in the world. There are four floating villages around Siem Reap: Kompong Khleang, Kompong Phluk, Chong Kneas, and Meychrey, home to a bird sanctuary. Kompong Khleang[32] is considered the largest and most authentic, and Chong Kneas the least authentic but most visited due to its proximity to the city.

Floating Villages

4.3
(7,842)

Floating Villages (Khmer: ភូមិលើទឹក) are traditional settlements on Tonle Sap Lake, where houses are built on stilts or float with the rise and fall of the water. The communities rely on fishing and seasonal agriculture. Visitors can explore the villages by boat and experience the unique lifestyle of people living in harmony with the lake’s ecosystem. There are four floating villages around Siem Reap: Kompong Khleang, Kompong Phluk, Chong Kneas, and Meychrey, home to a bird sanctuary. Kompong Khleang[32] is considered the largest and most authentic, and Chong Kneas the least authentic but most visited due to its proximity to the city.

Phnom Kulen National Park

4.3
(7,842)

Phnom Kulen National Park (Khmer: ឧទ្យានជាតិភ្នំគូលេន) is a sacred mountain located northeast of Angkor. It is considered the birthplace of the Khmer Empire, where King Jayavarman II declared independence in the 9th century. The site features waterfalls, riverbed carvings known as the “Valley of 1000 Lingas,” reclining Buddha statues, and ancient temples. Today, it remains both a historical site and a popular pilgrimage destination.

Kbal Spean

4.3
(7,842)

Kbal Spean (Khmer: ក្បាលស្ពាន), also called the “River of a Thousand Lingas,” is an archaeological site in the Phnom Kulen area. It is a carved riverbed with numerous lingas, yoni, and depictions of Hindu deities such as Vishnu and Lakshmi. The carvings, dating from the 11th–12th century, symbolize fertility and prosperity of the Angkor civilization. The site is reached by a short trek through the forest.

Angkor National Museum (Khmer: សារមន្ទីរជាតិអង្គរ) is an archaeological museum in Siem Reap, Cambodia. It is dedicated to the collection, preservation and presentation of Angkorian artifacts, also to provides information and education about art and culture of Khmer civilization, with collections mainly dated from Khmer Empire's Angkor period circa 9th to 14th-century. Most of the artifacts are discovered in and around the Angkor archaeological sites nearby.
